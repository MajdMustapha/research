   All 7 positions are US technology stocks. A tech-sector downturn (regulation, valuation compression, rate hikes) impacts 100% of the portfolio. Today's -3.66% day demonstrates this: every single position declined simultaneously.

2. **No Asset Class Diversification**
   - Bonds: 0%
   - Real Estate / REITs: 0%
   - Commodities / Gold: 0%
   - International Equities: 0%
   - Cash: 0.4%

   For a 20-25 year time horizon, having zero fixed income or alternative assets removes all downside cushioning.

3. **Extreme Position Concentration**
   META and MSFT together represent nearly 50% of the portfolio. A single company-specific event (regulatory action, earnings miss, leadership change) could cause outsized losses.

4. **Negligible Cash Buffer**
   Cash of $135.64 (0.4%) provides no dry powder for buying opportunities during drawdowns and no emergency liquidity.

5. **Unrealized Loss of -$7,051**
   This represents approximately an 18% decline from cost basis, suggesting recent entries at higher prices.

6. **High Correlation**
   All holdings are highly correlated with the Nasdaq-100. The portfolio essentially behaves as a leveraged bet on US mega-cap tech. The 8.178 SPX delta confirms significant directional exposure.

## Recommendations

### Priority 1 — Reduce Concentration Risk
- **Cap individual positions at 10-15%** of portfolio value. META and MSFT are each ~25%, double a prudent maximum.
- Consider trimming the top 4 positions and redirecting proceeds into underrepresented sectors.

### Priority 2 — Add Sector Diversification
Consider adding exposure to:
- **Healthcare** (e.g., UNH, JNJ, LLY)
- **Financials** (e.g., JPM, BRK.B, V)
- **Consumer Staples** (e.g., PG, KO, COST)
- **Industrials** (e.g., CAT, HON, UNP)
- **Energy** (e.g., XOM, CVX)

### Priority 3 — Add Asset Class Diversification
For a 20-25 year horizon:
- **10-20% Bonds / Fixed Income** — Provides ballast during equity drawdowns (e.g., BND, AGG, TLT)
- **5-10% International Equities** — Reduces US single-country risk (e.g., VXUS, EFA, VWO)
- **5-10% Alternatives** — Gold (GLD), REITs (VNQ), or commodities for inflation hedging

### Priority 4 — Build Cash Buffer
- Maintain 5-10% in cash/money market to deploy during corrections. Current $135 cash provides essentially zero flexibility.

### Priority 5 — Consider Index Core
- An S&P 500 (VOO) or Total Market (VTI) index fund as a 30-50% core holding would provide instant diversification across 500+ stocks and reduce single-stock risk, while keeping a satellite allocation to high-conviction individual picks.
